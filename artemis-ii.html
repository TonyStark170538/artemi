THE MISSION

Every code below carries a deeper story. Click any entry to expand — the Narrator will read it aloud.

EM-2
Original Mission Designation
Before NASA renamed the program "Artemis" in 2019 — after Apollo's twin sister in Greek mythology — this mission was Exploration Mission 2. The name change was deliberate: Artemis would land the first woman on the Moon.
▶
8.8M
Pounds of Thrust at Liftoff
SLS Block 1 generates 8.8 million pounds of thrust — 15% more than Saturn V. That reserve power is saved for Artemis III, which needs more delta-v to insert into lunar orbit for a landing.
▶
5000°F
Orion Heat Shield Temperature
Re-entering from the Moon at 39,000 km/h, Orion's AVCOAT shield hits 5,000°F — half the Sun's surface temperature. Artemis-1 revealed unexpected charring patterns studied before crew flight.
▶
1202
The Apollo 11 Alarm Code
Seconds before Apollo 11 touched down, a 1202 alarm triggered — a guidance computer overflow. Flight controller Steve Bales cleared it in 90 seconds. Without that call, there is no Moon landing.
▶
66100
Lunar Sphere of Influence (km)
At 66,100 km from the Moon, the spacecraft crosses the boundary where the Moon's gravity dominates Earth's. Apollo 8 astronauts described this as a moment of profound silence: the Moon now owns you.
